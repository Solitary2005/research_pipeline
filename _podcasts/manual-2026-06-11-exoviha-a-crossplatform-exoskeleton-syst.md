## Paper

**Exo-ViHa: A Cross-Platform Exoskeleton System with Visual and Haptic Feedback for Efficient Dexterous Skill Learning** — Xintao Chao, Shilong Mu, Yushan Liu et al.

## Summary Card

### Motivation
Imitation learning for dexterous manipulation is hindered by data collection systems that fail to balance acquisition efficiency, consistency, and accuracy. Traditional approaches often lack task realism and haptic feedback, leading to poor transfer between human demonstrations and robot deployment.

### Method
Exo-ViHa is a 3D-printed exoskeleton system integrating a SLAM camera, motion capture glove, and wrist-mounted camera to collect end-effector pose, hand movements, and visual data from a first-person perspective. It provides real-time haptic feedback and supports various dexterous hands and robotic arms, enhancing demonstration consistency and cross-platform compatibility.

### Key Results
Experiments demonstrate that Exo-ViHa significantly improves the success rate and efficiency of data collection for dexterous manipulation tasks compared to existing systems. The system achieves higher task realism and consistency between demonstrations and actual robot deployments.

### Takeaways
Exo-ViHa offers a practical, low-cost solution for scalable dexterous skill learning by combining first-person data collection with haptic feedback. Its cross-platform design and 3D-printed modularity could accelerate imitation learning research and real-world robot deployment.
