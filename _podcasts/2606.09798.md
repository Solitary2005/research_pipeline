## Paper

**[SynManDex: Synthesizing Human-like Dexterous Grasps from Synthetic Human Pre-Grasps](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09798)** — Yanming Shao, Zanxin Chen, Wenwei Lin et al.

## Summary Card

### Motivation
Dexterous grasp synthesis must reconcile human functional intent with robot-specific kinematic and contact constraints. Direct transfer of human grasps to robotic hands often fails due to embodiment mismatch, leading to interpenetration, mis-contact, or unreachable poses. A unified pipeline is needed that preserves semantic affordances while ensuring physical validity on high-DOF robotic hands.

### Method
SynManDex is a three-stage pipeline: (1) an object-conditioned diffusion model generates digital human pre-grasps as affordance-aware proposals; (2) a robot-native optimizer retargets and refines these into force-closure grasp keyframes with minimal penetration; (3) a task-specific planner integrates the keyframes into executable arm-hand trajectories. The key innovation is decoupling functional proposal generation from robot-specific physical optimization, using human pre-grasps as priors rather than final contact labels.

### Key Results
SynManDex achieves 86.4% grasp stability, 4.67/5 human-likeness (93.4%), and 80.7% simulation success on a 36-DOF bimanual platform. Real-robot experiments yield 25/30 (83.3%) successes on tabletop manipulation tasks. The pipeline supports diverse prehensile tasks including tea pouring, photo taking, and flute playing via VLM agents.

### Takeaways
This work demonstrates that human pre-grasp priors significantly improve robot-native grasp synthesis without requiring direct contact transfer. The staged formulation offers a scalable paradigm for generating physically valid, human-like dexterous manipulation data, enabling downstream policy learning with minimal real-world data. It bridges semantic affordance understanding and embodiment-specific optimization for high-DOF robotic hands.
