## Paper

**[Hand-in-the-Loop: Improving Dexterous VLA via Seamless Interventional Correction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15157)** — Zhuohang Li, Liqun Huang, Wei Xu et al.

## Summary Card

### Motivation
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for dexterous manipulation suffer from compounding errors due to high-dimensional action spaces and contact-rich dynamics, leading to distribution shift over long horizons. Interactive Imitation Learning (IIL) can mitigate this via human takeover, but direct teleoperation takeover causes abrupt command discontinuities, or 'gesture jumps', that destabilize grasps and cause failures in bimanual tasks.

### Method
Hand-in-the-Loop (HandITL) proposes a seamless intervention method that blends human corrective intent with autonomous policy execution. It introduces optimization-based relative hand retargeting that maps incremental fingertip motion (not absolute pose) to the robot hand, and a velocity-based shared-control interface for the arms that injects residual twists into policy-predicted commands, avoiding gesture jumps and preserving contact-rich manipulation.

### Key Results
HandITL reduces takeover jitter by 99.8%, grasp failures by 87.5%, and mean completion time by 19.1% compared to direct teleoperation takeover. When used to collect on-policy intervention data for fine-tuning, policies trained with HandITL data outperform those trained with standard teleoperation data by 19% on average across three long-horizon dexterous tasks.

### Takeaways
HandITL demonstrates that seamless, low-discontinuity human intervention is critical for effective IIL in high-DoF dexterous manipulation. The approach enables robust policy refinement from on-policy correction data, suggesting that careful interface design—rather than just data quantity—is key to scaling interactive learning for contact-rich, long-horizon tasks.
